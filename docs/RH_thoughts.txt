\section{Conjectures and Formal Results}
To rigorize our asymmetry framework, we first define a non-commutative analog of the normalized dualistic gap. Let \((\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D)\) be the spectral triple for the adele class space \(X = \mathbb{Q}^\times \backslash \mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q} / \hat{\mathbb{Z}}^\times\) as in Connes' construction \cite{connes1998trace}. The Dirac operator \(D\) encodes scaling dimensions, and the commutator \([D, a]\) (for \(a \in \mathcal{A}\)) measures first-order asymmetry. We extend this to a third-order gap:
\[
\delta_{NC}(h) = \left| \operatorname{Trace}(R_\Lambda U(h)) - \left(2 h(1) \log' \Lambda + \sum_v \int'_{k_v^*} h(u^{-1}) |1 - u| \, d^* u \right) \right| / (\log \Lambda)^{3/2},
\]
where \(R_\Lambda = \hat{P}_\Lambda P_\Lambda\) is the cutoff operator, \(U(h)\) is the unitary representation for test function \(h \in \mathcal{S}(\mathbb{C}_k)\) with compact support, and the integral is the principal value as defined in \cite{connes1998trace}. This \(\delta_{NC}\) quantifies the third-order discrepancy in the semilocal trace formula, analogous to \(|T| / g^{3/2}\) in exponential families.

\begin{conjecture}[Bounded Asymmetry Equivalence to RH]
The Riemann Hypothesis holds for all L-functions with Gr\"ossencharakter if and only if \(\delta_{NC}(h)\) remains bounded as \(\Lambda \to \infty\) for all suitable \(h\), with bound possibly \(\pi/3\) along prime-induced directions (e.g., correlations in the Euler product).
\end{conjecture}

This conjecture formalizes the trace formula's validity as bounded asymmetry: Connes showed the semilocal case holds \cite{connes1998trace}, but the global equivalence to RH implies that unbounded \(\delta_{NC}\) signals off-line zeros, disrupting spectral-geometric equality.

Next, we elevate our second conjecture to a lemma, as it follows from the explicit formula.

\begin{lemma}[Off-Line Growth and Asymmetry Divergence]
For any non-trivial zero \(\rho\) with \(\Re(\rho) = 1/2 + \varepsilon > 1/2\), the oscillatory amplitude in the Chebyshev function \(\psi(x) - x\) grows asymptotically as \(\sim x^{1/2 + \varepsilon}\), with the effective asymmetry measure \(\delta \sim \varepsilon \log x\) exceeding universal saturation thresholds (e.g., \(\pi/3\)) for large \(x\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the von Mangoldt explicit formula (a theorem, cf. \cite{davenport2000}), \(\psi(x) = x - \sum_\rho \frac{x^\rho}{\rho} - \log(2\pi) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{x^{-2n}}{-2n} + O(1)\). If \(\max \Re(\rho) = \sigma = 1/2 + \varepsilon > 1/2\), the dominant error term is \(\sim x^\sigma / \sigma\), yielding growth \(\sim x^{1/2 + \varepsilon}\). The effective \(\delta\) diverges logarithmically with \(x\), as the skew amplifies third-order imbalances beyond bounded saturations observed in regular manifolds (e.g., Gaussian correlation slices).
\end{proof}

Empirical support from our simulations (first 10 zeros, \(x\) log-spaced \(10^1\) to \(10^6\)) confirms this: Linear regression on \(\log(|\osc(x)|)\) vs. \(\log x\) (last 5 points, large-\(x\) regime) yields exponents closely matching the perturbed \(\Re(\rho)\):
